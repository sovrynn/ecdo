# ANCIENT INFRASTRUCTURE [(top)](http://example.com) [(top)](https://github.com/sovrynn/ecdo/blob/master/6-LITERATURE-MEDIA/corliss/)

- **Remarkable Roads**  
  The roads from ancient times display extraordinary engineering skills that challenge current understandings of early civilization capabilities. Some of these roads extend over vast distances and connect significant cultural sites, indicating a level of sophistication that warrants further investigation.

- **Mines**  
  Ancient mines reveal advanced techniques used in extraction and processing that seem to precede known historical advancements. The sheer scale and depth of these mines suggest an organizational capacity and technological proficiency that raises questions about the societies that built them.

- **Walls**  
  Defensive walls constructed by ancient cultures often feature intricate design and massive scale, hinting at a depth of strategic thinking. Their construction raises questions about the societal structure, resources, and technological prowess of the cultures that built them.

- **Mounds**  
  Many earth mounds show remarkable alignment with celestial bodies, suggesting they served a purpose beyond mere burial sites. This aligns with theories of ancient astronomical knowledge, while the construction methods used raise questions about the labor force and planning involved.

# Medicine Wheels [(top)](http://example.com) [(top)](https://github.com/sovrynn/ecdo/blob/master/6-LITERATURE-MEDIA/corliss/)

- **Anomaly of Limited Historical Records**: The earliest reference to medicine wheels was published in 1903, suggesting there may be earlier mentions yet to be discovered. This scarcity of documentation leads to speculation about the true historical significance and function of these structures.

- **Isolation of Knowledge Among Tribes**: Despite inquiries made by Simms to the Crow tribe regarding the "medicine wheel," none had visited it, and their knowledge was limited to hearsay. This isolation raises questions about the communication and preservation of tribal knowledge across generations.

- **Variations in Design and Function**: Each medicine wheel exhibits distinct features in its design, such as the number of spokes and structural complexity. This variation implies that different cultures or groups utilized the wheels for diverse purposes, challenging the idea of a singular use or meaning.

- **Disparity in Geographical Distribution**: The concentration of medicine wheels in Canada and their absence or rarity in certain regions suggest significant cultural or environmental factors influencing their creation. The great distances between known sites further complicate assumptions about shared cultural practices or builders.

- **Geometric and Astronomical Alignments**: The geometric similarities noted between certain North American medicine wheels and European stone structures raised questions about potential cultural diffusion. Astronomical alignments identified by researchers like Eddy indicate these structures may have served not just spiritual but also practical purposes related to timekeeping and navigation.

- **Cultural Absence in Modern Times**: Current Indigenous populations exhibit little awareness or connection to the historical significance of the medicine wheels, leading to speculation about their original cultural uses. Anthropologists must rely on legends and fragmented knowledge to reconstruct possible meanings, which may be influenced by time and the passage of traditions.

- **Skepticism of Astronomical Theories**: Some contemporaries express doubts regarding the utility of the alignments suggested for the Big Horn and Moose Mountain medicine wheels, criticizing the accuracy and reliability of their measurements. This skepticism highlights the ongoing debate within academia about interpreting ancient structures and their purposes.

- **Potential European Connections**: A reported stone wheel in Portugal with significant similarities to North American medicine wheels invites speculation about transatlantic cultural connections. Such claims are controversial and have not been widely accepted in archaeological circles, suggesting a need for further evidence to support or disprove these theories.

- **Woodhenges**  
  With the exception of the single recognized American woodhenge, astronomy was probably not the main objective of woodhenges; most seem to have been supports for large roofed buildings of some sort. The American woodhenge, at Cahokia, Illinois, was apparently only a simple calendar site and is, therefore, not anomalous.

- **Suggestions of Trans-Atlantic Cultural Diffusion**  
  Despite some similarities between the American and British woodhenges, they are separated in time by up to 3 millennia and also in purpose. Therefore, they cannot be considered evidence for early cultural diffusion across the Atlantic.

# General Observations [(top)](http://example.com) [(top)](https://github.com/sovrynn/ecdo/blob/master/6-LITERATURE-MEDIA/corliss/)

- **Astronomy and Woodhenge Structures**  
The axis of the original British Woodhenge was aligned with the summer Solstice, but the structure was not used for astronomy. Its posts probably supported a roof that precluded observations of the sky, indicating that it was a timber temple or meeting hall.

- **Wooden Structures in New World**  
The woodhenge at the American Cahokia site in Illinois was almost certainly devoted to astronomy, but it was basically a calendar site and not sophisticated enough to call anomalous. The great temporal gulf separating the Old World and New World woodhenges essentially bars any suspicions of trans-Atlantic influences. 

- **Independent Invention**  
Despite noted similarities between American Indian timber structures and those in Britain, this is likely a case of independent invention due to the time gap. The absence of anomalies across woodhenge structures removes the need for detailed analysis.

# Anomalies [(top)](http://example.com) [(top)](https://github.com/sovrynn/ecdo/blob/master/6-LITERATURE-MEDIA/corliss/)

- **Fresh-water mussels at Muscle Shoals**  
Muscle Shoals got its name from fresh-water mussels deposited by Indians, revealing a direct link between the area's natural features and its indigenous history. The presence of these shells showcases the utilization of local resources by early inhabitants.

- **Enormous shell mound at San Pablo**  
The shell mound at San Pablo was nearly a mile long, half a mile wide, and over 20 feet thick, indicative of significant cultural practices by local indigenous groups. Additionally, Indian skeletons and mummies, buried within the mound, demonstrate complex burial customs and social structures.

- **Massive shell deposits in the Aleutians**  
The Aleutians feature expansive shell deposits layered with mammalian bones, signifying a long period of accumulation over approximately 3,000 years. These findings highlight the interaction of marine and terrestrial ecosystems, and reveal insights into the Diets of past inhabitants.

- **Sambaquis along the coast of Brazil**  
Sambaquis, meaning "shell mountains," are complex mounds from which archeologists have recovered not only shells but also fish bones and human burials. At the Jabuticabeira-II site, a staggering 43,800 human burials emphasize the scale and significance of these structures in ancient Brazilian cultures.

- **Large sambaquis reaching heights of 60 feet**  
Studies reported by G. von Koenigswald revealed sambaquis that soared to exceptional heights, far exceeding typical structures of the time. This remarkable construction indicates advanced engineering skills and the cultural importance placed on these burial sites.

- **Shell Keys of Florida**  
The Shell Keys off Florida's southwestern coast are artificial islets predominantly composed of shells and include intricate features like walls and canals. Their complexity is likened to the Moundbuilder structures, suggesting advanced engineering capabilities of prehistoric cultures.

- **Curious Cairns of unusual characteristics**  
Cairns described in this collection boast unusual sizes and arrangements, making them worthy of investigation. Despite many serving practical purposes, some remain enigmatic, raising questions about their origin and function.

- **Extremely large cairns in New England**  
Cairns in New England's Indian cemeteries reach significant heights, with some measuring over 15 feet tall. The scale suggests a societal importance or ceremonial function, distinguishing them from the simple trail markers often attributed to cairn constructions.

- **The Great Stone Mound of Ohio**  
Ohio's Great Stone Mound, reported to be up to 50 feet high, faced significant destruction during the 19th century, yet remains a notable structure. Its impressive dimensions and the limited discoveries made during its dismantling challenge our understanding of the region's prehistoric inhabitants.

- **Alleged 200-foot-high cairn near Fort Laramie**  
A striking claim regarding a cairn near Fort Laramie suggests it rises 200 feet, which, if accurate, would be unprecedented. However, skepticism remains about these dimensions, indicating a potential for exaggeration or misinterpretation of its true size. 

- **Knocknarea cairn in Ireland**  
The cairn atop Knocknarea near Sligo, Ireland, is 35 feet high and 200 feet in diameter, believed to cover ancient tombs. Its impressive size and the surrounding legends add an air of mystery to its purpose and construction.

- **Barnenez cairn in Brittany**  
Barnenez in Brittany is an immense and complex cairn with burial chambers, showcasing a significant mixture of architectural styles akin to ziggurats. Its terraced design and unusual configuration challenge conventional categorization, emphasizing its historical importance.

- **Effigy cairns in North America**  
Effigy cairns found in Wisconsin depict animals and objects on a grand scale, often constructed with meticulous planning. While rare in North America, their existence suggests deeply rooted cultural expressions among indigenous populations.

- **Conical and pillar cairns in New England**  
These cairns, meticulously constructed from selected stones, showcase unique architectural features that differentiate them from other cairn types. Their age and purpose are unclear, raising intriguing questions about the societies that built them.

- **Burnt rock mounds in Texas**  
In Texas, burnt rock mounds display a specialized construction with densely packed angular rock fragments, possibly related to ancient cooking or ritual practices. The presence of soil and burnt remains suggests a particular cultural significance and utilitarian function.

  The existence of hundreds of granite spheres in southwestern Costa Rica, some up to 8 feet in diameter, presents questions regarding their origin and purpose. - **Natural Geological Processes**

- **North Dakota Stone Sphere Discovery**
