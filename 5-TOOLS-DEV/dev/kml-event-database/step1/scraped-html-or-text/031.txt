<p><b>Lake Chichancanab</b> from&#32;<a href="/wiki/Yucatec_Maya_language" title="Yucatec Maya language">Yucatec Maya</a>&#32;<i> "Little Sea"</i><sup id="cite_ref-Ramsar_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ramsar-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> is a lake in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> located in the northwest of the state of <a href="/wiki/Quintana_Roo" title="Quintana Roo">Quintana Roo</a>, in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mar%C3%ADa_Morelos_(municipality)" class="mw-redirect" title="José María Morelos (municipality)">José María Morelos</a>. It is also known as Laguna Chichancanab, Laguna Chicnancanab and Laguna de Chichancanab.<sup id="cite_ref-Getamap_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Getamap-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It has an approximate length of 30 kilometers from near the settlement of <a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Presumida&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="La Presumida (page does not exist)">La Presumida</a> to that of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kantem%C3%B3&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kantemó (page does not exist)">Kantemó</a>, in the same municipality. It is the largest lake on the <a href="/wiki/Yucatan_Peninsula" class="mw-redirect" title="Yucatan Peninsula">Yucatan Peninsula</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Popsci_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Popsci-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>The lake has a clay lake bed which seals the water off from the bedrock. The water level is therefore determined by the balance between rainfall and evaporation. The lakes waters are saline, with high concentrations of dissolved minerals. When the area undergoes drought for a sufficiently long period, <a href="/wiki/Gypsum" title="Gypsum">gypsum</a> precipitates and concentrates in the mud on the lake bed. In the last five thousand years, this deposit of gypsum has occurred at just one period which coincided with the start of the decline of the classical Mayan civilisation.<sup id="cite_ref-Popsci_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Popsci-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>On 2 February 2004, Lake Chichancanab was declared as <a href="/wiki/Ramsar_site" title="Ramsar site">Ramsar site</a> (ref. No. 1364), protecting an area of 1,999 hectares (4,940 acres). The site provides is utilised by at least 97 species of migratory birds. It is also inhabited by a diverse number of species of birds, mammals and reptiles which are protected under Mexican law, as well as being on the <a href="/wiki/IUCN_Red_List" title="IUCN Red List">IUCN Red List</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PP_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PP-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> An unusual feature of the lake is that it contains an inland population of the <a href="/wiki/Rhizophora_mangle" title="Rhizophora mangle">red mangrove</a> (<i>Rhizophora mangle</i>), as well as <a href="/wiki/Morelet%27s_crocodile" title="Morelet&#39;s crocodile">Morelet's crocodile</a> (<i>Crocodylus moreletii</i>) and various species of wading birds, although these have not been formally surveyed.<sup id="cite_ref-Ramsar_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ramsar-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>One almost unique feature of Lake Chichancanab is that different species of pupfish in the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Cyprinodon" title="Cyprinodon">Cyprinodon</a></i> coexist within the lake, in most parts of the world these species do not overlap in their range. There are, however, two notable exceptions one of which is Lake Chichancanab. Within the lake <i><a href="/wiki/Cyprinodon_maya" class="mw-redirect" title="Cyprinodon maya">C. maya</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cyprinodon_beltrani" class="mw-redirect" title="Cyprinodon beltrani">C. beltrani</a></i>, <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyprinodon_esconditus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cyprinodon esconditus (page does not exist)">C. esconditus</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cyprinodon_labiosus" class="mw-redirect" title="Cyprinodon labiosus">C. labiosus</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cyprinodon_simus" class="mw-redirect" title="Cyprinodon simus">C. simus</a></i>, <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyprinodon_suavium&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cyprinodon suavium (page does not exist)">C. suavium</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Cyprinodon_verecundus" class="mw-redirect" title="Cyprinodon verecundus">C. verecundus</a></i> are found, making up a <a href="/wiki/Species_flock" class="mw-redirect" title="Species flock">species flock</a>. The only other place where several <i>Cyprinodon</i> species are <a href="/wiki/Sympatry" title="Sympatry">sympatric</a> are lakes on <a href="/wiki/San_Salvador_Island" title="San Salvador Island">San Salvador Island</a> in the Bahamas. The sympatric <i>Cyprinodon</i> species in Lake Chichancanab have diversified to exploit different <a href="/wiki/Ecological_niche" title="Ecological niche">niches</a>. Pupfish typically feed on algae and <a href="/wiki/Detritus" title="Detritus">detritus</a>, however, in Lake Chichancanab, <i>C. maya</i> has become not only the largest species in the genus <i>Cyprinodon</i>, attaining lengths of up to 10&#160;cm (3.9&#160;in), but also the only species capable of preying on whole fish.<sup id="cite_ref-Martin2011_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Martin2011-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Among the <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> <i>Cyprinodon</i> species which were found in Lake Chichancanab, only <i>C. beltrani</i> and <i>C. labiosus</i> still occur in some numbers in their habitat, while the other species are almost <a href="/wiki/Extinct_in_the_wild" title="Extinct in the wild">extinct in the wild</a>. At least some of these, including <i>C. maya</i>, survive in captivity.<sup id="cite_ref-Martin2011_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Martin2011-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Martin2016_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Martin2016-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The primary reason for the decline of the endemic pupfish is <a href="/wiki/Introduced_species" title="Introduced species">introduced species</a>, notably the <a href="/wiki/Nile_tilapia" title="Nile tilapia">Nile tilapia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Tetra" title="Tetra">tetra</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Astyanax_fasciatus" class="mw-redirect" title="Astyanax fasciatus">Astyanax fasciatus</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Martin2011_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Martin2011-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Strecker2006_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Strecker2006-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
